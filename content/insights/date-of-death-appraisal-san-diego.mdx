# Date-of-Death Appraisal in San Diego
## Why It Matters for Heirs, Sellers, and Buyers

If you are dealing with an inherited property in San Diego, there is one document that quietly controls the entire strategy.
It is the date-of-death appraisal.
You may also hear time-of-death appraisal.
You may also hear retrospective appraisal.
Different names.
Same purpose.

A date-of-death appraisal establishes fair market value as of the owner’s date of death.
That number can influence taxes.
That number can influence estate administration.
That number can influence negotiations.
That number can reduce conflict between beneficiaries.

This guide is written for real San Diego trust and probate situations.
It is not theory.
It is not vague.
It is a working playbook.

## The simple definition

A date-of-death appraisal is a professional appraisal completed today.
The effective valuation date is in the past.
The effective date is the date of death.
The appraiser reconstructs the market as it existed on that date.
The appraiser estimates what the property would have sold for at that time.

This is different from pricing a home for sale today.
This is different from a Realtor CMA.
This is different from a Zestimate.
This is not a guess.
This is documentation.

## The fast answer

If you want the cleanest estate file, you typically keep two values separate.
You keep a date-of-death value.
You keep a current market value.
You use each one for its specific job.

1. Use the date-of-death value for basis support and estate records.
2. Use the current market value to price and sell the home today.

If you mix these up, people get confused.
If people get confused, they get emotional.
If they get emotional, deals get slow.
If deals get slow, deals get discounted.

## The tax angle people actually care about

Capital gains tax is based on basis.
Basis is the starting number used to measure gain.
When a property is inherited, the basis commonly steps up to fair market value at the date of death.
That is the step-up in basis concept in plain English.
A date-of-death appraisal can help document that value.

Here is a simplified example.

The owner bought the home for $200,000.
The home is worth $950,000 on the date of death.
The heirs sell later for $975,000.
The gain in this simplified scenario is much closer to $25,000 than $775,000.

Documentation matters.
A big appreciation gap makes sloppy documentation risky.
This is not tax advice.
This is common-sense risk management.
Your attorney and CPA should guide your exact tax path.

### What is a date-of-death appraisal

It is a professional valuation with an effective date in the past.
That date is the date of death.

### Is a date-of-death appraisal the same as a normal appraisal

It uses the same professional standards.
The difference is the effective date.
A normal appraisal values the property today.
A date-of-death appraisal values the property as of a past date.

### Who orders a date-of-death appraisal

Commonly.

Heirs.

### Do I need one if I sell immediately

Often yes.
Because basis documentation is tied to date of death.
Because estate records should be clean.
Because beneficiaries often need a baseline.

### Can I use Zillow instead of an appraisal

Zillow is an automated estimate.
It can be wrong.
It can be defensible only in casual contexts.
A professional appraisal is generally stronger documentation.

### What if the property changed after the date of death

That is common.
Vacancy can cause deterioration.
Water leaks can happen.
Maintenance can be delayed.
Repairs can be completed.

A retrospective appraisal aims to value the “then” condition.
Documenting “then” condition helps.

### What if we remodeled after the date of death

That remodel affects today’s value.
It does not change what the property was worth on the date of death.
Separate the concepts.
Date-of-death value is historical.
Current value is current.

### Does the date of death matter for property tax

Date of death can be a key reference point in certain contexts.
California rules can be fact dependent.
Coordinate with your attorney or CPA for your specific situation.

### What is stepped-up basis

It is the concept that inherited property basis commonly resets to fair market value at date of death.
That can reduce future taxable gain.
Documentation matters.

### What is the difference between probate and trust sale

A trust sale is often administered privately by a trustee.
A probate sale is administered through court procedures.
Specific requirements vary by case.
The appraisal concept can apply to both.
